Heritage

The Living Legacy

Built in 1895

Built during the final years of the 19th-century Golden Age of Sail, RONA evolved over more than a century while retaining her original character and pedigree.

Launched as Aura and later known to generations as RONA, this remarkable wooden yacht has crossed the divide between maritime history and living experience. Her story is one of reinvention: racing yacht, post-war rebuild, sail-training flagship, family vessel and, now, the foundation of an ambitious preservation vision in Southeast Asia.

From Aura to Rona

The surviving record shows a vessel that evolved without ever losing her essential character. Originally built by Stow & Sons at Shoreham-by-Sea in England, she received an engine in 1903 and eventually took the name Rona.

Following major damage in a 1937 gale, she lay through the war years before passing to Captain Linsay-Smith M.B.E. She was rebuilt six feet longer with her iconic spoon bow, sailing again as a ketch by 1951. Today, she remains a true heritage vessel, continuing a century-old legacy on the waters.
